Manual audit of differential completion examples

Inspect representative completion traces by hand before trusting the new vcomp
semantics completely.  Each trace should show previous boundary, previous
hidden, previous ports, event-local tiles, newly hidden vertices, consumed
ports, gained ports, next hidden, and next ports.  The goal is to make the
state transition visible rather than relying only on aggregate counts.

This audit is especially important around gained-port computation and base-case
connectivity.  The current implementation appears to use event-new hidden
vertices with event-local edges, which is probably the right interpretation, but
small examples should verify that no ports are missed and no invalid completion
realizations are accepted.
