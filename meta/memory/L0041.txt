Differential vcomp events are event-local

A vertex-completion event is not the same thing as one tile attachment.  One
completion event starts with a chosen boundary vertex and may recurse through
several tile attachments until that chosen vertex disappears from the boundary.
During those intermediate attachments, the implementation should accumulate
only event-local facts: which tiles were added during this completion and which
vertices became newly interior during this completion.  The committed vcomp
state, especially hidden and ports, should not be rewritten after every
intermediate attach step.

The actual state transition happens only at a candidate successful completion,
when the target vertex is gone.  At that point hidden is extended by the
newly-interior vertices from the event, consumed ports are old ports that became
hidden, and gained ports are new-boundary vertices adjacent by event-local tile
edges to the newly hidden constellation.  This event-local framing is the core
reason differential vcomp can remain small and why the connectivity check does
not need historical global tile data.
