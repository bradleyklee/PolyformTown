Live-boundary filtering changes the growth regime

After the differential vcomp update, the hat completion counts split into two
useful regimes.  All completions through n=15 appear to grow with a rough
effective exponential base near 1.6, while live-boundary-only counts through
n=20 grow much more slowly, around an observed effective base near 1.3.  The
data are still small and noisy, but the qualitative difference is large enough
to treat as a real signal.

This matters because live-boundary filtering seems to reduce the effective
branching rate, not merely remove a constant fraction of states.  If the
full tile-surround horizon is around the low-to-mid 30s, live-only lookahead may
remain in the low tens of thousands of states.  That would make memory pressure
negligible and leave runtime per expansion as the main practical bottleneck for
booting RL1 from RL0.
